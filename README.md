# Energy Systems Modelling and Macroeconomic Analysis

This repository contains academic projects, quantitative analyses, and structural modeling frameworks developed for the graduate course **Energy Systems Modelling** at Sharif University of Technology (under Dr. Hossein Khajepour & Dr. Moein Moeini).

## Core Course Focus
The curriculum explores the comprehensive integration and dynamic feedbacks between the **Supply Side** and **Demand Side** within macro-energy systems, establishing market clearance and policy impacts.

## Methodologies & Frameworks Covered
- **Comprehensive Energy Systems Integration:** Bridging supply-side cost minimization with demand-side utility maximization.
- **Input-Output (I-O) Analysis:** Utilizing Leontief paradox/matrices to evaluate direct and indirect accumulated energy demands across economic sectors.
- **Computable General Equilibrium (CGE) & SAM:** Analyzing economy-wide interactions, carbon/green tax structures, and price elasticities via Social Accounting Matrices.
- **Bottom-Up vs. Top-Down Coupled Frameworks:** Multi-sectoral interactions and resource constraints (Climate, Land, Energy, and Water interlinkages).

## Technical Implementations
- Mathematical formulation of market-clearing equilibrium equations.
- Life-cycle energy demand estimations and energy intensity trajectories using linear programming and matrix formulations.
